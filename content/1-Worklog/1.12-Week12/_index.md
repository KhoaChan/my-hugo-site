## V. Challenges, Error Handling & Solutions

In Week 12, the biggest challenge was ensuring that the report had no remaining issues before submission. Since the report included many different sections and both English and Vietnamese versions, I needed to check carefully to avoid missing content, formatting errors, or inconsistencies between versions.

One important issue was the consistency between written content and evidence. If a section mentioned screenshots, blog posts, or workshop activities, the related evidence needed to be placed correctly. Therefore, I reviewed all images, links, and captions to make sure readers could follow the content easily.

I also checked Hugo-related issues such as incorrect front matter, wrong weight, incorrect date, menu ordering errors, and broken image display. These issues could cause pages to appear in the wrong order or images not to show on the website. I handled them by checking each Markdown file, running Hugo locally, and refreshing the browser to verify the result.

Another challenge was making the content concise but still complete. Since the report had been written over many weeks, some sections had repeated ideas or inconsistent writing style. I shortened unnecessary parts, improved the wording, and kept the content focused on internship results.
