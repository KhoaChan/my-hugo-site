# Self-Evaluation of the Internship Process

During my internship in the **First Cloud AI Journey – Workforce Bootcamp 2026** program, I had the opportunity to study, practice, and experience a more realistic working environment in the field of cloud computing. This internship helped me strengthen the knowledge I had learned at school while also expanding my understanding of AWS, cloud infrastructure, security, networking, storage, CI/CD, and resource management.

Throughout the internship, I participated in many AWS labs related to IAM, VPC, EC2, S3, RDS, CloudWatch, Route 53, Load Balancer, Security Hub, WAF, KMS, and CodePipeline. Through these activities, I improved my ability to read technical documentation, work with the AWS Console, use AWS CLI, analyze errors, document implementation steps, and present results in weekly reports.

In terms of working attitude, I always tried to complete assigned tasks, actively learned more when facing difficulties, and recorded errors encountered during the practice process. I also realized that besides technical knowledge, I still need to improve my discipline, communication skills, and problem-solving mindset in order to adapt better to a professional working environment.

## Strengths

During the internship, I made clear progress in my technical knowledge related to AWS and cloud computing. I learned how to use many different AWS services, understood the role of each component in a cloud system, and was able to document my practice process in weekly reports.

I was also proactive in identifying errors, reading documentation, and retrying configuration steps when problems occurred. Completing weekly worklogs helped me improve my ability to summarize, present, and evaluate my own learning process.

## Areas for Improvement

Besides the results I achieved, I recognize that I still need to improve in several areas:

* **Improving discipline:** I need to practice following schedules, rules, and work plans more seriously, especially in a company or professional organization.
* **Improving problem-solving thinking:** When facing technical errors, I need to stay calm, analyze the issue step by step, and identify the root cause more clearly before choosing a solution.
* **Improving communication skills:** I need to learn how to present problems more clearly and concisely in both daily communication and work-related discussions.
* **Improving situation-handling ability:** When unexpected issues occur, I need to be more proactive in finding solutions and communicating with mentors or team members.

## Conclusion

Overall, the internship gave me valuable knowledge and practical experience in the field of AWS Cloud. I can see clear improvement in my technical skills, self-learning ability, and work documentation. However, I also understand that to develop further in a real working environment, I need to continue improving my discipline, communication skills, and problem-solving ability.

In the future, I will continue to strengthen the skills that are still limited, study AWS and cloud security more deeply, improve my system architecture mindset, and apply the knowledge I have learned to real projects.
