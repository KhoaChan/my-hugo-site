# Sharing and Feedback

After participating in the **First Cloud AI Journey – Workforce Bootcamp 2026** program, I had the opportunity to study, practice, and experience a real learning environment in the field of cloud computing. This was a very valuable experience for me because it not only helped me expand my technical knowledge of AWS, but also improved my working attitude, self-learning ability, problem-solving mindset, and documentation skills.

Through my participation in the program, I would like to share some personal feedback and suggestions as follows:

## I. General Evaluation

### 1. Learning and Working Environment

The learning environment in the First Cloud AI Journey program was friendly, open, and supportive. I felt that the atmosphere encouraged interns to learn actively, ask questions, and share difficulties during the practice process.

The working and learning space helped me focus better on technical topics, especially when practicing AWS labs. Being exposed to a more realistic environment also helped me understand how the knowledge learned at school can be applied in real work.

However, I think the program could include more communication sessions, group discussions, or team bonding activities so that members can understand each other better. This would make teamwork and collaboration more natural and effective.

### 2. Support from Mentors and Team Admin

The mentors and team admin provided strong support throughout the internship. When I had difficulty understanding a lab or troubleshooting an error, I could ask for guidance and receive helpful direction.

What I appreciated most was that the mentors did not simply give direct answers. Instead, they encouraged me to check the problem, analyze the cause, and find a solution by myself. This approach helped me improve my self-learning ability and problem-solving thinking.

The team admin also supported interns well by providing information, documents, schedules, and important announcements, which made the internship process more organized and convenient.

### 3. Relevance Between Internship Tasks and My Major

The internship content was highly relevant to my major in Information Technology, especially in areas such as systems, computer networking, security, application deployment, and cloud computing.

Topics such as IAM, VPC, EC2, S3, RDS, Load Balancer, CloudWatch, WAF, KMS, and CodePipeline are practical and useful in real-world cloud environments. Through this program, I not only strengthened my foundational knowledge from school but also learned many new topics that I had not practiced deeply before.

This helped me gain a clearer understanding of the work of a cloud engineer or cloud solution architect in real practice.

### 4. Opportunities for Learning and Skill Development

The program gave me many opportunities to develop both technical and soft skills. Technically, I practiced directly with the AWS Console, AWS CLI, infrastructure services, security services, storage, networking, monitoring, and CI/CD tools.

Practicing continuously each week helped me understand how different AWS services connect with each other to form a complete system. Besides technical skills, I also improved my ability to read technical documentation, take notes, write reports, present results, and evaluate my own learning progress.

These are important skills for working in the technology field.

### 5. Culture and Team Spirit

I found the culture of the program to be positive and supportive. Members were willing to learn, help each other, and respect each person’s development process. When I faced problems, I could discuss them with mentors or other members to find a solution together.

The team spirit during the learning process made me feel that I was not studying alone, but was part of a learning community. This motivated me to complete the labs, update my worklogs, and improve myself week by week.

### 6. Policies and Support for Interns

The program provided good support for interns during the learning and practice process. Having access to learning materials, lab guidance, practical environments, and technical sharing sessions was a very positive point.

I especially appreciated that the program allowed interns to work with real AWS services instead of only studying theory. This gave me practical experience and helped me better understand the technical requirements of deploying systems on the cloud.

## II. Additional Evaluation Questions

### 1. What satisfied me the most during the internship?

The thing I was most satisfied with was the opportunity to practice directly with many different AWS services and build weekly worklogs. Step-by-step practice helped me understand cloud infrastructure deployment more clearly, while writing reports helped me organize my knowledge and track my own progress.

I was also satisfied with the support from mentors and the team admin. When I faced difficulties, I was guided to find solutions by myself instead of becoming fully dependent on others.

### 2. What could the program improve for future interns?

In my opinion, the program could improve in several areas:

* Provide a clearer checklist for each lab, including required steps and screenshots/evidence that interns need to capture.
* Organize regular review sessions where mentors can give direct feedback on worklogs or internship reports.
* Add more experience-sharing sessions from people working as cloud engineers, DevOps engineers, or solution architects.
* Build more mini-project style labs so interns can better understand how multiple AWS services are combined in a complete system.

### 3. Would I recommend this program to my friends?

Yes, I would recommend this program to friends who are interested in cloud computing, DevOps, system administration, or cybersecurity. The program provides practical knowledge, hands-on experience, and a useful introduction to the AWS ecosystem.

For Information Technology students, this is a good opportunity to experience real cloud environments, develop system thinking, and prepare better for future jobs after graduation.

## III. Suggestions and Expectations

### 1. Suggestions to Improve the Internship Experience

I would like to suggest several improvements to make the internship experience even better:

* Provide a standard report or worklog template so interns can write documentation more easily.
* Add a short session on how to capture screenshots, store evidence, and present practice results clearly.
* Organize weekly demo sessions where interns can present what they have completed.
* Provide short mentor feedback after each stage so interns can understand their strengths and areas for improvement.
* Add more project-based labs, such as deploying a complete website with database, load balancer, monitoring, and CI/CD.

### 2. Expectation to Continue Similar Programs in the Future

If I have the opportunity, I would like to continue participating in similar programs in the future. I realize that I still have many areas to learn, especially system architecture, cloud security, DevOps, containers, microservices, and AI integration on AWS.

Continuing to participate in such programs would help me gain more practical experience, improve my technical skills, and prepare better for my future career path.

### 3. Other Feedback

Overall, I consider the First Cloud AI Journey program to be very valuable for Information Technology students. The program helped me approach cloud knowledge in a practical way, with specific labs, a real practice environment, and support from mentors.

I hope that in the future, the program will continue to expand with more advanced practical topics, especially real application deployment, system security, DevOps pipelines, containerization with Docker/Kubernetes, and AI integration into cloud systems. These topics would be highly useful for students who want to develop in cloud engineering or solution architecture.

## IV. Conclusion

After participating in the program, I feel that I have gained a lot of knowledge and practical experience, both technically and professionally. I now have a clearer understanding of AWS, cloud infrastructure deployment, account security, resource management, and technical reporting.

I would like to sincerely thank the mentors, team admin, and the First Cloud AI Journey program for giving me the opportunity to study, practice, and develop myself. The experience I gained from this program will be an important foundation for my continued learning and for applying cloud knowledge to real projects in the future.
