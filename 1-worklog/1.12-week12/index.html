Week 12 Worklog

I. Executive Summary

After completing the overall system architecture design in the previous week, in Week 8 I continued moving into the technical design refinement stage before starting the first implementation activities of the internship topic. The main objective of this week was to review the entire architecture, analyze each system component in more detail, and identify how each module could be implemented during the development process.

During the research process, I continued referring to AWS Documentation, AWS Prescriptive Guidance, and several Video-on-Demand architecture models on AWS to evaluate whether the selected architecture was suitable. Through self-review and analysis, I adjusted several components to improve scalability, reduce dependencies between services, and make the implementation process more practical.

At the same time, I started designing the data model for Amazon DynamoDB, building a list of business APIs expected to be implemented through Amazon API Gateway and AWS Lambda, and defining the data flow between system components.

By the end of the week, I had completed the basic technical design documents, creating a foundation for moving into the system implementation stage in the following week.

II. Strategic Objectives

After defining the overall architecture, the objective of Week 8 was to make the technical components more specific in preparation for system development.

The main objectives included:

  • Review and refine the overall architecture based on research and analysis.
  • Design the data model for Amazon DynamoDB.
  • Identify business APIs and the communication method between the Frontend and Backend.
  • Prepare the project structure and divide the system into functional modules.
  • Build a detailed implementation plan for each development phase.
  • Ensure that design decisions meet requirements for scalability, performance, and operational cost.

Through these objectives, I aimed to create a consistent technical foundation before starting the first development activities of the system.

IV. Detailed Work Execution & Analysis

In Week 12, I focused on completing the final version of my internship report. This was the final stage of summarizing the entire internship process, checking all report sections, and preparing the report in a submission-ready state.

At the beginning of the week, I reviewed the full internship report, including Worklog, Proposal, Blogs Posted, Events Participated, Workshop, Self-Evaluation, and Feedback. This review helped me identify the final adjustments needed before completing the report.

Next, I completed the Sharing and Feedback section. This section focused on my internship experience, evaluation of the working environment, mentor support, learning opportunities, and personal contributions to the program. It helped the report express my personal perspective after participating in the internship.

After that, I performed final content editing, including spelling, grammar, technical terms, wording, and consistency between the Vietnamese and English versions. Repeated or unclear sections were adjusted to make the report more professional and easier to understand.

I also checked all screenshots, image links, and visual evidence used in the report. The purpose was to ensure that images were placed correctly, displayed properly on the website, and supported the written content effectively.

Besides the report content, I tested the Hugo website locally, including the navigation menu, internal links, image paths, section order, and page display. After checking everything, I prepared the final version for GitHub deployment.

Finally, I summarized the entire internship process, completed the final internship report, and prepared the submission-ready version for the instructor.

V. Challenges, Error Handling & Solutions

In Week 12, the biggest challenge was ensuring that the report had no remaining issues before submission. Since the report included many different sections and both English and Vietnamese versions, I needed to check carefully to avoid missing content, formatting errors, or inconsistencies between versions.

One important issue was the consistency between written content and evidence. If a section mentioned screenshots, blog posts, or workshop activities, the related evidence needed to be placed correctly. Therefore, I reviewed all images, links, and captions to make sure readers could follow the content easily.

I also checked Hugo-related issues such as incorrect front matter, wrong weight, incorrect date, menu ordering errors, and broken image display. These issues could cause pages to appear in the wrong order or images not to show on the website. I handled them by checking each Markdown file, running Hugo locally, and refreshing the browser to verify the result.

Another challenge was making the content concise but still complete. Since the report had been written over many weeks, some sections had repeated ideas or inconsistent writing style. I shortened unnecessary parts, improved the wording, and kept the content focused on internship results.

VI. Professional Reflections

Week 12 allowed me to summarize the entire internship process, from learning AWS fundamentals, practicing basic services, researching Serverless architecture, to completing the final internship report. Through this process, I realized that I had improved both my technical knowledge and documentation skills.

Completing the final report helped me understand the importance of recording progress, saving evidence, and organizing content by stages. Without regular documentation, completing the report at the end of the internship would take more time and could easily miss important information.

In addition, checking the Hugo website, GitHub deployment, and Markdown files helped me improve my technical documentation management skills. I better understood how a website-based report should be organized, tested, and deployed so that readers can access it conveniently.

Overall, Week 12 was the final completion and summary stage. It allowed me to evaluate what I had learned, what I had completed, and what experiences I could apply to future work.

VII. Final Completion Plan and Post-Internship Direction

After completing the internship report, I will continue checking the final submission version to ensure that there are no remaining issues with content, images, or formatting. If I receive feedback from the instructor, I will make further adjustments to improve the report.

In addition, I will save all documents, source code, evidence images, and technical notes created during the internship. These materials will serve as useful references for future study, academic projects, and personal projects.

After the internship, I plan to continue strengthening my AWS knowledge, especially in Serverless services, security, databases, and cloud application deployment. The experience gained from completing the report and building the system will become a foundation that helps me feel more confident when joining real-world projects in the future.
